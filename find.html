Finding and Enrolling in Early Education Programs in Oakland:

  • First- use our map tool to locate a service that is convenient for you.
  • Talk to other parents about their experiences; read materials from BANANAS on selecting the right choice for your child and your family.
  • Call BANANAS for information and referrals to different types of programs -- full or part time, different age groups, private, public, subsidized, etc. (510) 658-0381 and
  • If you think your family meets low income guidelines – or if you have a child with a special need -- call the following subsidized programs directly:
    • Oakland Unified School District: 510-273-1590, website
    • Oakland Head Start: 510-238-3165, website
    • Unity Council Head Start: 510-535-6102 website
    • Ask questions about the services and learning opportunities provided, where programs are located, and visit them.
    • Be prepared to complete paperwork and present documents such as children’s birth certificates, proof of family income, health and immunization records, and proof of residency (not citizenship or immigrant status).

    Families in transition or without permanent homes are welcome. You may be put on a waiting list. Don’t give up! Keep calling!

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What programs are included in this website?
  • All of the State subsidized early learning programs and all of the federally supported Head Start programs in Oakland. This includes part day and full day programs. Most serve 3 & 4 year olds; a few are for infants and toddlers. The website provides information on each program.
  • Do these programs charge a fee?
  • Head Start does not; the state subsidized programs may charge a small fee depending on your income.
  • What if I am not low income?
  • Head Start is open to some higher income families; call to find out if you qualify. Call BANANAS—510-658-0138 for referrals to private early learning programs.
  • What if I have a CalWorks or other Alternative Payment Voucher to pay for child care?
  • Ask the program if they will accept your voucher – or call BANANAS for referrals to private programs that accept vouchers (most do!).
  • What if my child has a special need?
  • Tell the program about your child; you can also call the Family Resource Network Oakland for assistance ((510) 547-7322 or www.frmoakland.org)
